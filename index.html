MSc Research

Predicting cold starts before they happen.

Thesis · NCI · 2026

Low-Latency Cold-Start Optimisation for Serverless ML Inference

Cold starts are the silent latency killer of serverless ML: when a function hasn't run recently, the provider must spin up a container, load the model, then serve the request. My thesis builds a platform-agnostic ML framework that predicts when cold starts are likely and pre-warms functions to prevent them — across AWS, Azure, and GCP simultaneously.

A monitoring VM collected cold-start data across all three platforms running real models (ResNet50, DistilBERT, Random Forest). A logistic-regression classifier with cost-aware threshold tuning predicts risk and triggers pre-warming on a 3-minute cycle — with only 2.3% additional compute overhead.
